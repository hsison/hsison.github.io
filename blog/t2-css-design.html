Classes vs. IDs

What are classes and IDs? What is the best way I can utilize them in my code? If you are anything like me you are always asking why. Well here are my answers. Disclaimer: I am not a professional, just a nerd trying to learn some good old fashion HTML and CSS.

Classes and IDs are used for labeling and styling. For my ladies this is equal to how you style your hair and how that labels you as an individual. Ask yourself do you want the label "girl in the office who never does her hair" or "damn look at her locks". Think of your "elements" as hairstyles, each one has different labeling and styling. Moving on from hair lets get into it.

First of all, the two most important things you should know are:
1) IDs are used for things that appear ONCE per page.
2) Classes are used for things that appear several times per page.

When naming IDs and classes you are given the freedom to label every single element differently on your page if you choose, although I would not recommend that for cosmetic purposes.
Remember, simplicity never goes out of style.
In your external CSS page you can reference your IDs and classes very simply.

Because the classes and IDs are typically used for personal review that gives you the freedom to name elements classes or IDs anything you want within reason. You want to make sure these names make sense to YOU and general sense to others incase you want to share the code one day.
