How To Get The Answers You Want

Have you ever asked a question in class and the teacher kind of tilts their head and takes a moment to think about what you've just asked them? You most likely suck at asking questions...but that's okay! Most people aren't very good at asking questions so they can get the answers they want.

Here are some tips and tricks to becoming a question asking pro!

Here are a few examples of poorly asked questions:

Bad: C# Math Confusion
Good: Why does using float instead of integer give me different results when all of my inputs are integers?

Bad: [php] session doubt
Good: How can I redirect users to different pages based on session data in PHP?

Bad: android if else problems
Good: Why does str == "value" evaluate to false when str is set to "value"?

How to ask questions:

Research the question yourself first:

Is this an easy question that could've been answered on a simple Google search?

Explain your misunderstanding:

What caused you to get confused? Was it the way the question was asked? Do you need the content to be explained to you again?

State what you know:

What part do you understand? To improve clarity, let the other person know which parts you understand fully.

State what you don't know:

Explain which part confuses you.

Be specific:

Make sure you are specific and concise.

Use correct grammar:

Make sure you use correct grammar to improve clarity.

Use specific language:

Instead of saying, "What is this code doing to this?"
Ask something more specific such as, "What will this method output when called?"

Keep your question simple:

Ask one question at a time and try to make it as simple as possible.

Use either open ended or closed questions:

Open ended questions usually start with phrases like "why" and "tell me more about"

Closed ended questions usually start with phrases like "when" and "who"

Practice, Practice, Practice

The only way you become great at doing something is to do it often.
Ask, ask, ask.
Don't be afraid to ask questions often.Use some of these tips and tricks to improve your question asking skills.
Now go into the world and ask away!
