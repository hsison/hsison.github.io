The Mind

What is your thinking/learning style?
According to Anthony F. Gregorc’s Mind Styles article, “No one is a "pure" style. Each of us has a unique combination of natural strengths and abilities.”
Even though I did this personal thinking style test in the prep work before starting Phase 0, I really didn’t understand how powerful it could be to understand yourself/thinking style on a deeper level.

My Experience

So far my experience in Phase 0 has been both frustrating and rewarding.
I have to be honest and say it’s leaned more toward the frustrating. I am a very visual/audible learner and have always struggled with reading comprehension on my own. If I watch a video or listen to someone explain something to me I grasp the concepts particularly faster. When reading on my own I find myself loosing concentration in the middle of reading a paragraph or two and throughout my life that has been very challenging.
During week one of Phase 0 we were given a challenge where we were supposed to research time management and productivity.
During that assignment I came upon the video How to create a Habit(with Charles Duhigg) .This amazing video talks about how focus, in itself, is a habit. He says, “Focus increases with practice. You need to recognize and keep track of your practice, so you can convince yourself you’re getting better at it”.
He talks about how making the conscious choice to focus, for even ten minutes at a time, that your focus remains very high. He also mentions the importance of breaks. Give yourself time to not be focused.
After watching this video in week one, in combination with the Pomodoro Technique, my focus has improved by probably 40%. To me this is a huge deal. I know that with practice and time my focus will be close to 100% and I am so excited for that.

My Thinking Style

According to the Mind Styles article, for the most part I am a Concrete Random Thinker. The article says Concrete Random Thinkers are experimenters and that I am.
This explanation of Concrete Random thinkers was absolutely spot on for me.
This learner likes:
• experimenting to find answers
• take risks
• use their intuition
• solving problems independently
They learn best when:
• they are able to use trial-and-error approaches
• able to compete with others
• given the opportunity to work through the problems by themselves.
What's hard for them?
• Restrictions and limitations
• Formal reports
• Routines
• Re-doing anything once it’s done
• Keeping detailed records
• Showing how they got an answer
• Choosing only one answer
• Having no options

Growth vs. Fixed Mindset

In the video above with Charles Duhigg he talks about a book written by Daniel Coyle called “The Talent Code.” Although I’m not an avid reader, I really want to expand my mind and be successful in my future so I will be reading “The Talent Code” by Daniel Coyle. Also I will be reading Charles Duhigg’s book “The Power of Habit. I will also continue to practice focus and use the Pomodoro Technique in my daily studies.
